Start here

A map of the app, and what to ignore at first

trajecktory has nine places you can go. That sounds like a lot, and on your first morning it is. So here is the honest version: you only need two of them to start. The rest fill in as your search grows, and they will still be here when you need them. Nothing breaks if you ignore a tab for a month.

Start with these two
Today · your plan for the day, and your to-do list.
Pipeline · every role you are tracking, and where each one stands.
Add these as you go
Follow-Ups when things go quiet · Interview when you get one · Recruiters, TA Outreach and LinkedIn SSI once you start talking to people · Insights once you have enough history to learn from.
Why the order matters Every tab except Today is built from the same list of roles. Insights cannot tell you what is working until you have applied to a few things, and Follow-Ups has nothing to chase until you have sent something. Working the first two tabs well is what makes the others useful later, so starting narrow is not cutting corners. It is the shortest path.
Pro tip Press / anywhere to jump into the search box, and Ctrl + K to open a command list that can take you to any tab by name. Once you know where you are going, you will stop using the menu entirely.

The rhythm

Your day, in about twenty minutes

A job search fails from going quiet, not from a bad application. The point of this loop is that it is small enough to do on a bad day. Twenty minutes, most days, beats three hours once a fortnight.

1
Open Today
See your blocks and anything overdue. Start the first block and let the timer run.
→
2
Find and score
Run the Workflow steps in the sidebar. New roles land scored, ready to judge.
→
3
Decide and apply
Read the strong ones in Pipeline. Apply to the good ones, close the rest honestly.
→
4
Chase and log
Clear anything in Follow-Ups, then log what you sent so tomorrow knows about it.
What the app will chase you about, and when
7 working days after you apply, with no reply
3 working days between interview rounds
5 working days after someone replies
14 working days for a talent-acquisition contact
Weekends are not counted. Nothing is sent automatically, ever: these only decide when a role appears in Follow-Ups asking you to take a look.
Why bother logging it Every number this app shows you (what is working, which sources are worth your time, where you tend to lose people) is calculated from the statuses and touches you record. Skip the logging and the app does not just lose data, it starts giving you confidently wrong advice about your own search. Two minutes at the end of a session is what keeps the rest honest.
♥ Missing a day is fine. There is no penalty, no lost progress, and the streak counter on Today is there to encourage you, not to judge you. Skipped days do not break it: only days where you had blocks scheduled and did not finish them.

Tab by tab

Today: what to work on right now

Two columns. On the left, your time blocks for today, each with a timer. On the right, your to-do list. The number beside Today in the menu is unfinished blocks plus anything overdue, so your whole goal is to make it disappear.

The Today sub-tab
Tick a block's box when it is done. To-dos take a priority (High, Med, Low) and an optional due date, and the chips filter them: Open, Today, Overdue, Done, All.
The Schedule sub-tab
Set your week once. Name each block, pick days with MWF, TT, Weekdays or Every day, set a time and length, then Save schedule. Four sensible blocks are already there to rename.
Pro tip Do not invent to-dos from memory. Open a role in Pipeline, go to its Notes tab, and use Add a to-do there. It arrives on Today already tagged with that company, so when you sit down tomorrow you know exactly which role the task belongs to without going to look.
Why blocks beat a to-do list alone A list tells you what to do but never when, so the hard tasks slide. A block is a promise to a time, and the timer makes starting the only decision you have to make. That is why the outreach block matters most: it is the one people skip, and it is the one that produces replies.

Finding roles

The Workflow: how new jobs arrive

Down the left, under the menu, is the Workflow. These are the steps that go and find jobs for you. You run them one at a time, top to bottom: click a step, wait for it to finish, click the next. There is deliberately no "run everything" button.

The steps on a Claude plan setup.
  1. API Scan is free and uses no AI at all. It asks the Greenhouse, Ashby and Lever job boards directly what is open at the companies you track.
  2. Triage has Claude read the most promising ones cheaply and score them. They appear as a list underneath. This is the next page.
  3. Merge Tracker is free. It moves the scored rows into your Pipeline tab.
  4. Verify Actionable is free. It quietly drops postings that have since closed.
  5. Health Check is free. A final all-clear.
⚠ Do not skip Merge Tracker. Scoring a role writes its report, but Merge Tracker is what puts the row in your Pipeline. Skip it and the Pipeline looks empty, and you will reasonably assume the scan failed when it actually worked.
ⓘ "0 new" is normal, not a failure. A scan might report "0 new (of 14,338 found: 14,264 off-title, 28 duplicates, 43 geo-blocked, 3 stale)". That means it looked at everything and none of it matched your titles, your location, or was still open. To see more, widen your target roles in Setup. The first run takes a few minutes; later ones are much faster.
Why one step at a time The scoring steps run on your Claude plan, and running several at once trips the usage limits faster than it saves time. Running them in order also means each step gets the previous one's output, so a failure part-way leaves you with everything that already worked, rather than an unclear mess.

Finding roles

Triage: deciding what is worth reading

Triage is a cheap first pass. Claude skims the postings and gives each a rough score, so you can spend your real attention on the few that deserve it. The scores appear as cards under the Workflow steps.

Scored cards, best first.
Three things you can do with a card
Deep dive writes the full report for that one role, and it lands in your Pipeline.
open JD shows you the posting itself, so you can judge for yourself.
✕ dismiss removes it for good.
A triage score is written as ~4.6 with a tilde, and its box has a dashed edge. That is the app being honest that this is a quick guess, not the considered score you get from a Deep dive.
ⓘ Triage is a Claude plan feature. Add an API key and bill to it, and this panel disappears entirely, replaced by a batch Evaluate step. Page 19 lays out that trade honestly.
Pro tip Be ruthless with ✕ dismiss. A dismissal is remembered, so that job will not come back on the next scan, or the one after. A short list you actually work beats a long list you avoid.
Why not just read everything A full evaluation is the expensive part, in time and in Claude usage. Triage exists so that cost is only ever spent on roles that already look right: skim everything, deep dive a handful, apply to fewer.

Pipeline, sub-tab 1 of 4

Overview: how the whole search is going

The Pipeline tab has four sub-tabs across the top: Overview, Active, All and Analytics. It opens on Overview, which is the one-glance answer to "am I actually getting anywhere".

The four counters
Total Tracked, Pending Decision (scored, not yet actioned), Response Rate (compared against a 22% benchmark), and Avg Score.
Pending Roles
Each row nudges you by age: scored today, still fresh, decide soon, then getting stale. Work this list down and the tab badge clears.
⚠ The Apply button here does not apply. On a Pending Roles row, Apply and Review do exactly the same thing: they open the role so you can read it. Applying happens inside, and page 11 covers it. Only Skip changes anything from here, and it closes the role immediately with no confirmation.
Why the response rate matters more than the score A high average score only means the scanner is finding roles that match your profile. The response rate tells you whether employers agree. If scores are high and replies are not, the problem is usually the CV or the level you are targeting.

Pipeline, sub-tabs 2 and 3 of 4

Active and All: the working list

Active shows the roles still in play. All shows everything, including the ones you closed. Same table, different slice, and that is genuinely the whole difference.

Reading a row
Comp shows one midpoint figure, or Not Stated when the employer did not say. A row flagged ↻ overdue is one the Follow-Ups engine thinks has gone quiet. Click any row to open its full report.
Getting it out
Export CSV, on the Active sub-tab only, gives you a spreadsheet of the list as it is currently filtered. Useful if someone asks what you have applied to.
Pro tip If the table looks wrong and clearing the filters does not fix it, check the search box at the very top of the window. Clear resets the chips and the score filter but deliberately leaves your search term alone, so a forgotten search is the usual culprit. Press / to jump straight to it.
💡 Rows with a dashed score and no number. Those are triage results that have not been deep dived yet, sitting in the list as a reminder. They are not clickable, and their buttons live inside the row itself.

Pipeline, sub-tab 4 of 4

Analytics: where things are going wrong

Numbers about your active roles. This is not the same as the Insights tab: Analytics shows you the measurements, Insights asks Claude what they mean. Use this one when you have a specific question.

What each panel answers
Source Effectiveness · which job boards are worth your time
Time in Stage · where things stall
Archetype Conversion · which kind of role replies
Pipeline Flow · how roles moved, end to end
Comp Positioning · are you aiming too high or too low
Interview Stage Funnel · which round loses you
ⓘ Two honest limits. Roles where the employer did not state a salary are simply not plotted in Comp Positioning, so that panel describes the postings that disclosed, not all of them. And every panel here counts active roles only, so its response rate can legitimately differ from the one on the Overview sub-tab, which counts everything.
Why this needs a few weeks before it says anything Every panel is a rate, and a rate over four applications is noise. Give it twenty or thirty logged roles before you change strategy on what it shows you. Until then, treat it as a curiosity rather than a verdict.

Reading a result, part 1 of 2

The report: is this role worth it?

Click any row and the full report slides in from the right. It has eight tabs, and a ninth appears once you have applied. The first four answer the only question that matters before applying: is this a good use of my time?

The Overview tab, top to bottom.
What you are looking at
The stage track across the top shows where this role stands and lets you move it. Then TL;DR, four headline numbers, and the score broken into its parts.
Overview · CV Match
CV Match lines up each thing they asked for against the evidence in your CV, marks it strong, moderate or weak, and lists your gaps with a way to handle each.
Comp · Legitimacy
Comp compares the posted pay to your target and your walk-away. Legitimacy is the scam check: real company, real role, real posting, with reasons.
Pro tip Read the score breakdown, not just the score. A 4.2 built on a weak seniority match is a completely different decision from a 4.2 held back only by an unstated salary. The breakdown takes ten seconds and it is the difference between applying well and applying often.
Why the Legitimacy tab exists at all Job boards carry a real number of fake and stale postings, and applying to one costs you a tailored CV and your personal details for nothing. Anything below a clean verdict is worth thirty seconds of your own checking before you hand over an address and a phone number.

Reading a result, part 2 of 2

The rest of the report, and the one tab you type into

The first four tabs help you decide. These five help you act: what to say, what to change, and what you promised to do next.

The Notes tab: the only one you write in.
Notes
Add a to-do at the top sends a task to your Today tab, already labelled with this company. Below it, saved notes build a history of the conversation.
The other four
Interview · the story to lead with, your STAR stories, and the awkward questions with answers.
Customize · the exact changes to make to your CV and LinkedIn for this role.
Contacts · talent-acquisition people here, and a way to find more.
Follow-up · appears once applied; draft and log your chasing here.
ⓘ Customize does not edit anything. It tells you what to change and why. You make the edits yourself, in your own CV and LinkedIn profile. Nothing is altered on your behalf.
Pro tip The moment a call ends, open Notes and write three lines while it is fresh: what they pushed on, what you promised, and what happens next. In six weeks, when the same company comes back, this is the difference between a warm reply and a blank stare.
Why to-dos belong here and not in your head A task created here arrives on Today tagged with the company, so tomorrow you know which role it belongs to without going to look. That tag is the whole point: it is what turns "chase someone" into "chase this specific person about this specific role".

Applying

The four buttons, and what each really does

At the bottom of a report you have decided to act on, there are four buttons. They look similar and they are not. Two open the job posting, two do not. Three mark the role as applied, one does not. This page is worth two minutes.

⚠ "Applied" means logged, not submitted. None of these buttons sends anything to an employer. trajecktory writes your documents and records that you are going after this role. You still have to fill in the company's own application form yourself. The confirmation reads "✓ Applied to Northwind Analytics", which is easy to read as "it went". It did not.
Tailor CV · writes a version of your CV aimed at this role, and opens the posting in a new tab so you can apply. Marks the role Applied.
Claude Apply · the same, plus draft answers for the form's written questions. Also opens the posting and marks it Applied.
Already Applied · for when you applied by yourself. Writes nothing, opens nothing, just marks it Applied.
Cover Letter · writes a letter and stops. Does not open the posting and does not change the status.
ⓘ Give it a moment. A short message shows while it works ("Generating tailored CV..."), and the buttons are disabled until it finishes. The status only changes once the writing is done, so if it fails the role stays where it was. Your finished files appear as links right there, and land in Documents\trajecktory resumes.
Pro tip Use Cover Letter on its own before you commit. It costs you nothing, changes nothing, and reading how the letter argues your case is the fastest way to find out whether your CV really supports this role. If the letter is thin, the application would have been too.
Why it never submits for you Every application a human reads costs someone's attention, and a form submitted without you seeing it is one you cannot stand behind in the interview. The tool does the tedious half and deliberately stops at the point where judgement starts.

Keeping track

Moving a role along, and closing it honestly

Every role sits on a nine-stage track, from Evaluated to Offer. Keeping it accurate takes about five seconds per update, and it is what makes every other number in the app mean something.

Moving forward
The big button at the bottom always names the next step: Mark Responded, then Move to Phone Screen, then Move to 1st Interview, and so on up to Accept Offer. You never have to remember the order.
Closing it
The quieter buttons beside it close the role: Rejected, No Response, Not a Fit, Closed. On a role you have not applied to yet you get Skip instead.
⚠ Closing a role happens immediately, with no "are you sure". The report closes and the role leaves your active list. Nothing is lost: it is still in the All sub-tab, and a closed report has a Reopen button that puts it back. But there is no undo prompt, so read the button before you click it.
Why "No Response" and "Rejected" are different buttons Being turned down after three rounds and never hearing back at all are different outcomes, and lumping them together hides the most useful thing you can learn. When you close a role that got somewhere first, trajecktory quietly records how far it reached, so a rejection after a final interview still counts as a strong result in your figures rather than a failure.
Pro tip Update the status the moment the email arrives, not at the end of the week. The follow-up clock starts from the status change, so a late update means trajecktory nudges you late, and by then the thread has gone cold on its own.

Not dropping the ball

Follow-Ups: the tab that saves applications

Most applications do not die from rejection. They die from silence, on both sides. This tab watches every role you have sent and tells you which ones have gone quiet long enough to be worth a nudge.

Warm threads
A human has engaged, or you have an email address for them. These are worth chasing, and the badge in the menu counts only these.
Applications out
Sent into a form with no human attached yet. A calm ledger of what is outstanding, deliberately quiet so it does not nag you daily.
Why a LinkedIn-only contact counts as cold LinkedIn limits how many messages you can send, so a LinkedIn-only thread is not reliably actionable and the app will not pretend otherwise. If a role matters, finding an email address is what moves it from the quiet list to the one that actually nudges you.
Pro tip Done for now mutes a thread indefinitely, which is the honest option for a role you have given up on but do not want to mark rejected. Use it freely: a Warm threads list you trust is one you will keep opening, and a list full of dead threads is one you will start ignoring.

Talking to humans

Recruiters: the relationships that compound

External recruiters work many roles at once. One good relationship can put you in front of jobs that were never posted, which is why this tab is worth time even in a week when you apply to nothing.

ⓘ Nothing here finds recruiters for you. You add them: Directory, then Import CSV, using the Template button to get the right columns. A spreadsheet is the fastest way in, and it is deliberately the only way, because who you work with is your call and not something a scanner should guess.
The stages
Each contact moves Not Contacted to Drafted, Sent, Replied, Meeting Scheduled, Connected. Logging a message advances it for you.
The drafting
Open anyone and use Draft first outreach. Claude writes it in your CV's voice. You copy it, send it yourself, then click I sent this.
⚠ Nothing is ever sent for you. Every draft on this tab produces text with a Copy button. You send it from your own email or LinkedIn, then tell trajecktory it happened. That is why the log buttons exist, and why the numbers are only as good as your honesty about them.
Why the app nags you below 40% The Overview coaching lines are benchmarks, not judgements. Below roughly 40% of your list contacted, the constraint is reach rather than message quality, so the fix is more outreach. Once you are above that and replies are still thin, the fix is a better opener. The tab tells you which problem you actually have.

Talking to humans

TA Outreach: a person, not a form

Talent acquisition are the in-house recruiters at the companies you are applying to. This tab keeps one list of them, per company, and helps you write to them. It is a single screen, no sub-tabs.

Why this is the highest-value habit in the whole guide An application submitted through a form is one of hundreds in a queue. A short, specific note to the person who actually reads that queue gives your name a face before the sift. It is not a guarantee of anything, but it is the difference between being a row in a list and being someone they have heard of. The motion is: apply, then work the contact. Not instead of applying, and not weeks later.
Pro tip Do this from inside the role, not from this tab. Open the job in Pipeline, go to its Contacts tab, and use Find contacts at ... there. The contact arrives already attached to that company, and when you log the message it is recorded against the application too, so you never enter the same thing twice.
ⓘ The Reconcile button, in one line. It compares your contact list against your live applications, offers to archive contacts at companies you are no longer chasing, and finds contacts at companies where you have none. Changes are saved as soon as you apply them, so review the list before confirming.

Talking to humans

LinkedIn SSI: being visible before you apply

SSI is a score LinkedIn gives you, out of 100, for how well you use the platform professionally. This tab does not read it automatically. It gives you somewhere to record it, and a plan for moving it.

The four parts of the score, and what moves each
Establish Brand · your profile and what you post
Engage with Insights · commenting and sharing
Find Right People · who you search for and follow
Build Relationships · connections and conversations
Why aim for three to five touches a day All four parts reward being consistently present rather than occasionally loud. Three to five small actions a day (a considered comment, a share with a line of your own, one connection request) hits every part of the score without becoming a second job. A single burst once a month moves none of them. This is also why the Networking block exists on your Today tab.
Pro tip Add the hiring managers and leaders at your target companies as influencers, not just famous names. Commenting usefully on a post by someone who will later see your application is worth more than a hundred likes on a celebrity's thread.

When you get one

Interview: prep, and a board for the call itself

A company appears here once you move it to Phone Screen or beyond and it has prep notes written. Until then it reads No interviews on deck, which is correct rather than broken.

Prep
Your research written out: what to say first, your hero story, what to avoid, what to ask them. A Sections list moves you around a long document.
Live
A stripped board for during the call. Click a prompt, its answer appears in big type positioned under your webcam, so your eyes stay up.
💬 Adding a round uses the handoff you already know. Click + New round, give it a number and optionally a short description, and trajecktory hands you an instruction to paste into the Claude Code tab, exactly like the Launchpad steps in Guide 2. Nothing exists until you run it, so clicking the button on its own is always safe.
Pro tip Use Print the night before and take the Cram sheet on paper. One or two pages of what to say, your hero story, what to avoid and what to ask. It survives a dead battery and a bad connection, and reading it on paper the morning of works better than scrolling.

Learning from the data

Insights: what your own search is telling you

Everything else in trajecktory records what happened. Insights is where you ask Claude to read all of it at once and say what it means. It never runs by itself, so it costs nothing until you press the button.

The four sub-tabs, once it has run
Overview · the headline, plus a short focus list for the week
What's not · where you are losing, each with a concrete fix
What's working · what is earning replies, so you do more of it
Recommended moves · in priority order, each small enough to finish today
⚠ It is nearly useless below about five applied roles. Every claim it makes is a rate, and a rate over three applications is noise. It will tell you when a sample is thin rather than pretending, but the honest advice is to leave this tab alone for your first fortnight.
Why run it monthly, not daily Nothing here changes hour to hour, and re-running it clears the week's focus list you have been ticking off. Once a month, or after a run of rejections you cannot explain, is when it earns its cost. Results are saved, so opening the tab any other time is free and instant.

Optional, and often unnecessary

The API key: what it changes, and what it does not

Everything in this guide works on your Claude plan alone. An Anthropic API key is an optional extra that buys you throughput, not capability. Here is the honest comparison, so you can decide whether you need one at all.

On your Claude plan alone
Triage, and a scored card list you review by hand.
Deep dive one role at a time, or paste a job link to evaluate it on the spot.
Every AI draft: CVs, cover letters, outreach, follow-ups.
The full Insights analysis.
Model choices shown as fast, balanced, deepest.
With a key, billed to the key
Four extra buttons: Agent Scan, Liveness Gate, Evaluate (Batch) and Expand Coverage.
Evaluate writes 10 reports per run instead of 5, and works on several at once.
The triage card list disappears.
Model choices shown as dollar estimates per run.
⚠ Adding a key does not replace signing in to Claude. Scanning and evaluating still run through your Claude sign-in either way, so the one-time Sign in to Claude in the sidebar is required regardless. Your key is a fallback for the rare moment that sign-in is unavailable.
What actually gets charged to the key Only Insights and the writing drafts reliably bill your key. Triage, Agent Scan and Evaluate run on your Claude subscription even with a key saved, which is why the app labels each step subscription or API key rather than leaving you to guess. If you were expecting a key to move the heavy scanning off your plan, it does not.
♥ Most people should not bother. If you are applying to a handful of well-chosen roles a week, the plan flow is the better fit and costs nothing extra. Consider a key when the batch size is genuinely your bottleneck.

Housekeeping

Controlling what you spend

In Setup, under Optional boosters, the Models & cost panel decides which Claude model runs each step. The defaults are already the cheap, calibrated ones, so the honest advice is to leave it alone until you have a reason.

⚠ The dollar figures are estimates, not your bill. They are worked out from token counts on your own machine, not from your Anthropic invoice. Since the scanning and evaluating usually run on your Claude subscription anyway, most of what you see here will never appear on an Anthropic bill at all.
Pro tip Leave Triage on Haiku. It is the cheapest model and it has been checked against the full scoring rubric for exactly this job, so paying more here buys you almost nothing. If you want to spend more anywhere, spend it on Evaluate, which writes the reports you actually read.
Why there is a "Bill to" switch at all If you have saved a key but want a quiet month, flipping billing to your Claude plan keeps the key saved and simply stops using it. You do not have to delete anything to stop spending, which means you can experiment without a one-way door.

Housekeeping

When something looks wrong

Almost everything that looks broken is one of these, and none of them lose your data.

"401 authentication_error" on a scan or evaluate. Your Claude sign-in expired. Click Sign in to Claude in the sidebar again, then retry the step. It is not a bug and nothing is lost.
The Pipeline is empty after scoring. Run Merge Tracker. Scoring writes the reports; Merge is what puts the rows in the tab.
A tab looks empty and you expected data. Check the search box at the top of the window. It keeps its term when you move between Pipeline, Follow-Ups and TA Outreach, and Clear does not clear it. Press / to jump there.
An update banner appeared. Click Update now. Your CV, tracker, reports and settings are never touched by an update, and there is a rollback if anything goes wrong.
A "Claude usage or limit pressure" notice. You have hit your Claude plan's rolling five-hour limit, usually from a heavy run. Wait a while and try again. Running the Workflow steps one at a time rather than back to back makes this rarer.
A role you applied to is still "Evaluated". The status only changes once the document generation finishes. If it failed, the role stays put on purpose. Look for the red message, then try again.
The score column shows a tilde and a dashed box. That is a triage guess, not a full evaluation. Run Deep dive on it for the real one.
Insights says the sample is thin. It is being honest. Come back after a few more applications have had time to get responses.
♥ Your data is yours, and it is plain text. Everything lives in ordinary files in your trajecktory folder: the tracker, the reports, your notes. Nothing is locked in a database, nothing is in the cloud, and you can open any of it without this app.
