## §1 — Mental Model: What This Loop Evaluates

> Final loops evaluate ACROSS panels collectively. Different from any single round. You need to be consistent across panels — interviewers compare notes.

The loop is evaluating:
1. **Consistency** — does the candidate tell the same story to peers, cross-functional partners, executives? Inconsistency is a hard signal of either fabrication or lack of substance.
2. **Range** — can they shift altitude? Can they go deep with a peer engineer AND high with an exec sponsor in the same day?
3. **Substance under multiple lights** — their hero story will get probed by 3+ people from 3+ angles. Does it hold?
4. **Collaboration signal** — would the team actually want to work with them? This is what peer panels are really for.
5. **Cultural and judgment fit at scale** — the executive panel is asking, "would I sign off on this hire?"

**Your job:** {one sentence — thread a consistent hero narrative across all panels, demonstrate range without losing identity, leave at least one specific artifact or framework that every panel mentions in debrief.}

**Do NOT:** {2–3 things — e.g., contradict yourself across panels, pretend to know things you don't (panels compare notes), front-load all your energy on panel 1 and run out by panel 4.}

## §7 — Hard Questions Prep

> Questions where the panel is genuinely probing for cracks. Different from screen "red flags" — these are sharper.

**"You've shown us a lot of {X}. Can you tell us about a time you failed at {X}?"**
> "{Real story. Real miss. Real reflection. Senior candidates lean INTO this question; junior candidates dodge.}"

**"What's the worst feedback you've ever gotten — and what did you do with it?"**
> "{One specific instance. The feedback verbatim. What you initially thought. What you did 3 months later.}"

**"How would your last team describe you in one word? And the next word?"**
> "{Two real words. Probably one strength, one trait that's a double-edged sword. Demonstrates self-awareness.}"

**"If we made you an offer today, what would your hesitation be?"**
> "{Honest. Specific. Not 'no hesitation' — that's a lie. Frame the hesitation as something the loop has actually addressed OR something you'd want to validate.}"

**"Why should we hire YOU over the other final candidates?"**
> "{The specific thing only you bring. Not 'I work harder' or 'I'm passionate.' A specific intersection of skills/experience/judgment that's defensibly rare.}"

## §8 — Questions to Ask (Categorized by Interviewer Type)

> Different questions for different interviewer roles. Prepare 2–3 per category.

**For peers / direct teammates:**
- What's your honest answer to "what's hard about working here"?
- Who on the team has grown the most in the last year, and what did they do?
- What's the team currently arguing about?

**For cross-functional partners:**
- Where does this team frustrate you, and where do you wish you had more from them?
- What does a successful collaboration with this team look like in practice?

**For executive sponsor / skip-level:**
- What's the board / leadership team asking this function about right now?
- What would have to be true in 2 years for you to look back at this hire as a great one?
- What's the biggest strategic shift you're anticipating, and how does this role show up in it?

**For the panel coordinator / repeat HM contact:**
- What's the timeline to a decision? And to a start date?
- What would a strong offer signal look like coming out of this?

## §9 — Logistics

> The mechanical things for a final loop are different. More moving parts.

- [ ] Schedule + room assignments confirmed with coordinator (or Zoom links tested for virtual)
- [ ] All interviewer names + titles + LinkedIn profiles re-skimmed morning of
- [ ] Travel + accommodation for on-site loops booked, with buffer time
- [ ] Outfit + backup outfit if on-site (coffee spill insurance)
- [ ] Phone charger + laptop charger + portable battery
- [ ] Notebook + 2 pens + water bottle
- [ ] Lunch logistics confirmed (if onsite — they usually feed you; ask about dietary needs in advance)
- [ ] Restroom + reset breaks built in — if loop is 4+ hours, ask coordinator for 10-min buffers between panels
- [ ] §0 cheat strip and §3 thread on a notecard you can reset to between panels
- [ ] Phone on Do Not Disturb but reachable
- [ ] Notify references — they may get called THIS week

## §10 — After the Loop — Same Day & Next Day

**Within 4 hours of the last panel:**
- [ ] Thank-you note to EACH interviewer separately. Not a form letter. Reference something specific from THAT interviewer's conversation. 3–5 sentences each.
- [ ] If there was a case / exercise, send any promised follow-up artifact within 24 hours
- [ ] Self-debrief in this file: per-panel — what landed, what didn't, what you'd say differently. Include any questions you got that you weren't prepared for.

**Day after:**
- [ ] Log full debrief in `data/applications.md` notes column for #{NNN}
- [ ] Update story-bank.md with any NEW stories you ended up telling, or refinements to existing ones
- [ ] If references requested: brief each reference verbally before they're called — name the role, the company, the 1–2 angles to reinforce
- [ ] Comp prep: write down your verbal-offer response + counteroffer framework BEFORE you're on a call where they share a number
